What Are Clementines?

Clementine is a citrus fruit, a tangor which is mainly a hybrid between the willow leaf mandarin orange and sweet orange, and was named as Clementine in 1902. From the outside, this citrus fruit has a deep orange color with a smooth and glossy appearance. Like tangerines, they can be peeled easily and are mainly separated into 7 to 14 segments. This citrus fruit is sweet and juicy in taste, but in comparison to orange, it is less acidic.

Clementines were initially grown at the citrus research center and later introduced to California commercial agriculture in 1914. Clementines are mainly of two types- seedless clementines and with seeds.

Seedless clementines mainly exist in North Africa, and it has no seeds or fewer seeds, it can’t pollinate on its own and needs to be cross-pollinated.

And on the other hand, there are clementines with seeds and as they have seeds so they can self-pollinate. They are also known as Monreal clementines, and on average they are bigger than seedless variety and are sweeter than them.
