25 Amazing Health And Beauty Benefits of White Pepper

  • by Bharat Sharma
  • May 20, 2021

Both white pepper and black pepper are the flowers of the same shrub and differ from each other in terms of taste, color, Aroma, processing technique, and as well as the benefits they provide. Like black pepper, white pepper is also native to South India. It is also known by some other names such as ‘safed mirch’ in Hindi, ‘Tella miriyalu’ in Telugu, ‘Velai melaku’ in Tamil, ‘Bili menasu’ in Kannada,’ Safed Mari’ in Gujarat, and ‘Paandri Miri’ in Marathi.

Health Benefits of white pepper

White pepper is an excellent source of many nutrients like vitamins, minerals, antioxidants, phytonutrients, polyphenols and dietary fibers, etc that provides numerous benefits ranging from better digestion, oral care, diabetes care, weight loss, cardiovascular issues, and so on. In this article, we will know about some of the major benefits of white pepper.

(1) Provides Anti-Inflammatory Benefits

Inflammation plays an important role in the healing of wounds and injury as it is our body’s way of dealing with injuries. It is during the inflammation, our body works on repairing the cells and tissues that get damaged during an injury or wound. During inflammation, our body also takes care of the foreign microbes that may worsen the injury.

However, inflammation is a double-edged sword, and our bodies do their best to keep inflammation in control. However, some inflammatory diseases such as arthritis, rheumatism, and gouts, etc. can interfere with this.

White pepper is very beneficial for providing relief from the pain and inflammation caused by inflammatory diseases such as arthritis, rheumatism, rheumatoid, gouts, asthma, and bronchial asthma. The anti-inflammatory properties of the white pepper are due to the presence of capsaicin in it.

(2) Natural pain-relieving agent

White pepper is a natural pain-relieving agent that provides relief from the muscular pain that one may experience during injury. This advantage of white pepper is due to the presence of capsaicin in it. Capsicum is a hot substance that is known for generating heat in the body.

The heat so generated by the capsaicin helps in the recovery of the damaged tissues of the body.

Because of the pain-relieving property of the capsaicin, it is added to pain-relieving gels and sprays.

(3) Prevents Cancer

White pepper is very beneficial for reducing the risk of various types of cancers due to the presence of antioxidants and other antioxidantal compounds like phytonutrients and polyphenols in them.

The antioxidants and the related compounds present in the white pepper fights with the free radicals in our body, stabilize them, and prevents them from causing oxidative damage to our cells and tissues, and thus reduces the risk of various cancers such as colon cancer, lung cancer, prostate cancer, and abdominal cancer, etc.

In research conducted by the University of Nottingham and the American Association of Cancer Research, it was found that the capsaicin present in the white pepper was effective for killing some of the cancer cells, and is very beneficial against prostate cancer.

This advantage of white pepper is because of the presence of capsaicin in it. Capsaicin in the white pepper accelerates the rate at which fat cells are burned by the body, and thus helps with weight loss. Because of its fat-burning abilities, it is common to add it to most weight loss medicines and solutions.

The dietary fibers in the white pepper also help with weight loss because they provide satiety to our stomach. In other words, it gives a feeling of fullness to our stomach, decreases overeating which is a common cause of obesity.

One teaspoon of white pepper contains around 7 calories of energy, which means when you consume them, you don’t need to worry about calorie overload.

Our Immune system plays an important role in keeping us healthy as it is the defense mechanism of our body that protects us from disease-causing microbes such as bacterias, viruses, fungus, and parasites, etc.

For healthy living, it is important to take good care of our immune system, and white pepper can help in doing the same.

By protecting white blood cells from oxidative damage, white pepper strengthens our immunity, and when our immune system is stronger, we are less likely to get infected by the cough, cold, flu, and fever, etc.

Other nutrients like vitamins and minerals in the white pepper also play a significant role in making our immune system better.

Difference Between Black And White Peppers

Both black pepper and white pepper are obtained from the pepper vine shrub and differ in the way they are processed.

Black pepper is originally green in color as it is the unripe fruit of the pepper vine. It is removed from the vine, and then cooked and dried to give it a black and shriveled crust. On the other hand, white pepper is the ripe fruit of the pepper vine. It is soaked in water for nearly 15-20 days, after which its skin begins to fall off, and the inner white seed of the fruit comes out which is dried and used either as a whole or as powder.

Both black pepper and white pepper offer a tangy and spicy flavor and are used to give a unique taste to the dishes. In comparison to white pepper, black pepper is spicier and has a citrusy taste with an earthy flavor. Contrary to this, white pepper has a sharp taste with a pungent aroma.

Both black pepper and white pepper are loaded with essential nutrients like vitamin A, vitamin C, vitamin K, zinc, iron, magnesium, potassium, and other compounds such as phytonutrients, polyphenols, and dietary fibers, etc. that provides amazing benefits. However, both these pepper differs from each other in the following ways.

(1) Difference In Aroma

In comparison to white pepper, black pepper has a pungent aroma.

(2) Difference In Processing

Both white pepper and black pepper are the fruits of the Piper Nigrum or pepper plant and differ from each other in the way they are processed. Black peppercorns are plucked from the pepper vine when they are almost ripe. They are then sun-dried to remove moisture, and this makes them wrinkly. This process makes the outer layer black. On the other hand, white peppers are plucked once they are completely ripe on the plant itself. After plucking, they are soaked in water for 10-15 days to soften their outer layer. This removes the outer layer of the fruit, and the inner white layer of the seed comes out. They are then sun-dried and can be used as a whole and even in powdered form.

(3) For The Sake Of Aestheticism

This difference has to do more with the visuals, and less with the properties or taste. White pepper is a preferred choice to give an aesthetic touch to white sauces, soups, and mashed potatoes, and other light-colored dishes. Adding black pepper to white dishes won’t look so appealing.

Similarly, if you are cooking something that involves dark colors then black pepper will blend into the mix perfectly in comparison to white pepper.

(4) Difference In Hotness

Both white pepper and black pepper are known for their heat-generating properties, but they differ from each other in terms of hotness. White pepper offers instant hot flavor, but black pepper releases hotness slowly on chewing.

Depending on your taste preference, you may find both white pepper and black pepper to be spicier than each other, but in reality, they both are equally spicy.

(5) Preference In Different Cuisines

Both black pepper and white pepper have the same origin, but they are preferred over each other depending on the cuisines. In many international and intercontinental cuisines, black pepper is the preferred choice. However, in Chinese, Thai and French cuisines, white pepper is preferred over black pepper.

(6) Amount Of Essential Oils

If black pepper and white pepper are to be differentiated on the grounds of essential oil, then black pepper will win without a doubt as it has more essential oil in comparison to white pepper for the same weight.

(7) Medicinal Properties

Comparatively, white pepper has more medicinal benefits than black pepper, and it is the reason it was widely used in the ancient Ayurvedic and Chinese therapies for prevention and/or treatment of various health issues such as malaria, constipation, nasal congestion, arthritis, food poisoning, joint pain, and cholera, etc.

(8) Difference In Odor

Black pepper has a strong and woody flavor because of its outer black layer that remains intact even at the processing time. On the other hand, the outer layer of the white pepper is removed at the time of processing, and it loses the scents that give a floral and woody odor to it.

In comparison to black pepper, white pepper has a mushy odor that develops during fermentation.

(9) Different Shelf Life

In comparison to white pepper, black pepper has a longer shelf life and can be stored in the kitchen for a long time without the worry of losing its aromatic flavor.

However, white pepper can’t be stored for long as it develops a bitter taste on rotting.

(10) Extent of Allergy

Both white pepper and black pepper show different levels of intensity to the people who are allergic to them.

If you are allergic to pepper as a whole, then you are more likely to react badly to black pepper in comparison to white pepper.

This is because in simple terms white pepper is just the seed of the pepper as its outer layer is removed. On the other hand, the outer layer of the black pepper is intact. The composition of black pepper is intense and may cause severe allergic reactions.
