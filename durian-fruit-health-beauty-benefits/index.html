33 Amazing Health & Beauty Benefits of Durian Fruit

  • by Bharat Sharma
  • November 13, 2021

Durian is an edible fruit belonging to the genus Durio. It is considered the “king of fruits” in Southeast Asian nations such as Malaysia, Indonesia, and Brunei.

Durian provides many health and beauty benefits as they are rich in vitamins, minerals, antioxidants, phytonutrients, and dietary fibers, etc.

Health Benefits of Durian Fruit

Some major health benefits of durian fruit are as follows.

(1) Excellent Source of Energy

Durian fruit is a wonderful snack that boosts energy for day-to-day activity and is a better alternative to caffeinated energy drinks.

They contain 147 calories per 100 grams and provide about 21 percent of the daily carbohydrate requirements.

Carbohydrates in durian provide a steady supply of energy, and dietary fibers in durian provide stomach satiety and control overeating.

(2) Reduce Stress And Anxiety

Unmanaged stress and depression can harm our physical, mental, and emotional health.

It is important to keep them under control and durian can help in doing the same.

Vitamin B6 in durian increases serotonin production in the body.

Serotonin is a neurotransmitter that relaxes our brain, calms our nervous system, reduces stress and anxiety, and helps with many other functions such as mood improvement, healing wounds, maintaining bone health, and so on.

Other nutrients such as magnesium etc in the durian also play a significant role in stress-busting by relaxing our nervous system.

(3) Enhances Strength of Bones And Teeth

The bone-strengthening minerals such as calcium, potassium, and magnesium, etc in durian strengthen our bones and teeth and protect them from damage.

Calcium is an essential mineral that promotes the growth and development of the bones and reduces the risk of bone disorders such as osteoporosis.

Potassium in durian improves bone density by neutralizing acid load and reducing the loss of calcium from the bones.

Magnesium, manganese, and other nutrients, etc in durians also play an important role in improving bone health.

(4) Promotes Digestive Health

Durian improves our digestive health due to the abundance of nutrients.

Vitamin C, vitamin E, and other relevant compounds such as phytonutrients, etc in durian protect our digestive system from free radical damage and reduce the risk of abdominal cancer, intestinal cancer, colon cancer, and pancreatic cancer, etc.

Because of its laxative properties, dietary fibers in durian improve our bowel movement, relieve constipation and provide relief from digestive disorders such as abdominal pain, gas, bloating, and flatulence, etc.

Potassium, magnesium, and B vitamins, etc in durian also help in improving our digestive health.

(5) Regulates Blood Sugar Level

With a low glycemic index of 49, durian slow down the rate at which sugar is released into the bloodstream prevents a sudden spike in blood sugar level, and helps in managing diabetes.

Dietary fibers in durian regulate the rate at which sugar gets absorbed by the bloodstream and keep blood sugar under control.

Despite the advantage, eat durians moderately as otherwise they may drop blood sugar below the healthy levels and cause excessive hunger, excessive sweating, dizziness, shakiness, fast heartbeat, and moodiness, etc.

If you take diabetes medication, add durian to your routine after consulting with your doctor to ensure there is no food-drug interference.

(6) Provides Relief From Hypertension

Hypertension or high blood pressure is a silent killer that affects millions around the world and is a leading cause of cardiovascular and other health problems.

Durian contains potassium, a natural vasodilator that relaxes our blood vessels, improves blood circulation, and provides relief from high blood pressure or hypertension.

However, too much potassium may drop our blood pressure below the healthy range and cause dehydration, fainting, nausea, irregular heartbeat, breathing difficulty, and vision problems.

To avoid the risk, eat durian and other potassium-rich foods moderately.

If you take blood pressure medication, consult your doctor before adding durians to your routine and ensure there is no food-drug interference.

Low sodium in durian also plays an important role in regulating blood pressure.

(7) Reduces The Risk of Anemia

Eating durians reduce the risk of iron induced anemia.

Iron-induced anemia is a blood disorder caused by the deficiency of iron in the body. It may cause pale skin, fatigue, irregular hearbeat, shortness of breath, dizziness, and chest pain, etc.

Durian contain iron, a trace mineral that enhance the production of red blood cells in our body.

Red blood cells carry oxygenated blood to our cells and tissues and nourish them.

This reduces the risk of iron-induced anemia and improves general well-being.

Durian are rich in powerful antioxidants such as vitamin C, vitamin A, and other relevant compounds such as flavonoids, etc. These compounds protect the red blood cells from free radical damage and improve their efficiency.

(8) Provides Aphrodisiac Benefits

The aphrodisiac properties of durian increase libido and improve sexual health.

Vitamin B6 in durian regulaes sex hormone and increases libido by controlling raised prolactin level and regulating the production of estrogen, red blood cells, testosterone, dopamine and serotonin.

It also benefits women by increasing estrogen, a prime sex hormone in women.

Other nutrients such as vitamin A, vitamin C, magnesium, and B vitamins, etc in durian also play an important role in improving our sexual health.

(9) Reduce Sleep Disorders

Quality sleep plays an important role in keeping us healthy as our body performs many important functions during sleep. Not getting proper sleep can cause sleep disorders such as insomnia and increase the risk of other health problems like obesity, hypertension, and poor cardiovascular health.

Durian fruit contains tryptophan, an amino acid that converts to serotonin hormone and induces a feeling of relaxation, and happiness. It calms our nervous system and reduces stress, a major cause of poor sleeping habits.

Durian enhances the production of melatonin hormone or sleep hormone. This hormone manages our sleep cycle and provide other benefits such as keeping eyes healthy, treating stomach ulcers and heartburn, increasing human growth hormone, etc.

Other nutrients such as calcium, potassium, iron, and B vitamins, etc in durian also play an important role in improving our sleep quality.

(10) Protection Against Cancer(s)

Durian fruit is an excellent source of vitamin C, vitamin A, and other antioxidant compounds such as flavonoids, etc.

These compounds protect our cells from free radical damage and reduce risk of many cancers such as colon cancer, abdominal cancer, lung cancer, pancreatic cancer, breast cancer, and so on.

Free radicals are unstabilized ions that stabilize by stealing electrons from neighboring molecules and doing so cause oxidative damage to them.

(11) Promotes Weight Gain

Durian fruits are high in calories ( 147 calories per 100 grams) and are beneficial for underweights who would like to gain some pounds.

They are rich in carbohydrates and provide energy at a steady rate, and can be used as a nutritious snack.

Healthy fats in durian fruit improve our heart health and promote weight gain.

Durian is also rich in dietary fibers that provide stomach satiety and controls ovrereating.

This way it helps with weight gain while keeping eating habits in control.

(12) Boosts Immune System

The immune system is the defence mechanism of the body as it prevents and treats microbial infections, diseases, infections, and wounds.

Vitamin C, vitamin A, and other powerful antioxidants in durian protect our immune cells or white blood cells from free radical damage and boost our immunity.

This strengthens our immune system, and reduce the risk of getting a cough, cold, fever, and flu, etc.

Vitamin C further strengthens immunity by increasing white blood cells production in the body.

Because of their antimicrobial properties, durian fruit reduce the risk of bacterial, viral, and fungal infections.

Potassium, calcium, magnesium, iron, and B vitamins in durian also plays a major role in increasing our immunity.

(13) Metabolizes Carbohydrates

Durian fruit is rich in carbohydrates, the main source of energy in our body.

The complex carbohydrates present in the durian fruit release energy at a steady rate and are better compared to simple carbs that release instant energy, raise blood sugar levels, make us hungry sooner, and lead to overeating.

Additionally, vitamin B1 (thiamine) present in the durain fruit improve carbohydrates metabolism and provide us energy.

However, too many carbs may cause weight gain, increase risk of diabetes and cardiovascular problems.

Eat durian fruit in moderation to avoid the risk.

(14) Good For Brain

Durian fruit is good for the brain and improves our neurological health.

They contain many antioxidants such as vitamin C, vitamin A, and other relevant compounds that protects our brain cells or neurons from free radical damage.

This reduces the risk of neurological disorders such as Parkinson’s disease, Alzheimer’s disease, dementia, and amnesia, etc.

Nutrients such as potassium, magnesium, folate, iron, and B vitamins, etc in durain improve our neurological health by providing cognitive benefits.

This includes sharper memory, better concentration, increased alertness, better recall, reduced confusion, etc.

Being a vasodilator, potassium in durian relaxes our blood vessels and improves blood circulation to neurons. Iron increases oxygenated blood level in the brain and helps it to perform optimally.

Folate or the folic acid in durian help in synthesis of neurotransmitters, cellular detoxification, and proper functioning of nervous system.

(15) Eating Durians During Pregnancy And Nursing

Durians play an important role in maintaining healthy pregancy due to the abundance of vitamins, minerals, antioxidants, phytonutrients, and dietary fibers, etc.

Vitamin A, vitamin E, vitamin C, and other antioxidants in durian fruit protect the fetus from free radical damage.

Folate or folic acid in durian promotes the brain development of the fetus and reduces the risk of birth defects like neural tube defects and spina bifida.

Durian regulates blood sugar level and reduces the risk of gestational diabetes often experienced during pregnancy.

Dietary fibers in durian reduce the risk of digestive problems during pregnancy.

Other nutrients such as potassium, magnesium, zinc, calium, iron, and B vitamins, etc in durian also play an important role in maintaining a healthy pregnancy.

However, in large doses fibers in durian may cause diarrhea, stomach discomfort, abdominal pain, and other gastrointestinal problems.

Durian may also react with other medications prescribed by your doctor during pregnancy.

To stay safe, consult your doctor before eating durians during pregnancy and ensure there’s no complication or drug interference involved.

Nursing mothers should also be cautious and consult their doctor before adding durians to their routine.
