35 Amazing Health & Beauty Benefits of Dragon Fruit

  • by Bharat Sharma
  • May 13, 2021

Dragon fruit or pitaya fruit/pitahaya fruit is a delicious fruit that has a slightly sweet taste and is widely popular in the regions of Central America, South America, and Asia. Its texture is between that of kiwi and apple. It contains essential nutrients like vitamins, minerals, antioxidants, phytonutrients, and polyphenols and, dietary fibers, etc that provide many health and beauty benefits.

Health Benefits of Dragon Fruit

Source- Pixabay.com

Following are the major health benefits of dragon fruit.

(1) Manages Cholesterol Level

Dragon fruit helps to manage cholesterol levels as it contains dietary fibers that reduce LDL cholesterol from the body and minimize the risk of atherosclerosis.

Atherosclerosis is a condition that hardens and narrows our arteries and is caused by the accumulation of plaque and fat deposits in the arteries. It is a leading cause of several cardiovascular problems like heart attack, heart stroke, and coronary artery disease.

Vitamin C and other antioxidnatal compounds like phytonutrients and polyphenols etc in dragon fruit also play a significant role in cholesterol management.

(2) Aids In Detoxification

The detoxifying compounds like dietary fibers and vitamin C in dragon fruit reduce toxins, pollutants, impurities, and other unwanted substances from our bodies.

To some extent, it reduces the burden of detoxification from our vital organs like the liver and kidneys and allows these vital organs to focus on other functions they need to perform. Some of these functions are bile juice production, metabolism of fat, protein and carbohydrates, and hormone production etc.

(3) Improves Digestive Health

Dragon fruit is good for the stomach as it contains dietary fibers, natural laxatives that improve bowel movement, aids in stool elimination, and thus relieve constipation and other gastrointestinal issues like abdominal pain, irritable bowel syndrome, gas, bloating, and flatulence, etc.

Antioxidants like vitamin C, vitamin A and antioxidantal compounds like phytonutrients, polyphenols, and flavonoids, etc in dragon fruit protect our digestive system from free radical damage and minimizes the risk of colon cancer, abdominal cancer, pancreatic cancer, intestinal cancer, and stomach cancer, etc.

Other nutrients like zinc, iron, magnesium, and folate, etc also improves our digestive health.

(4) Regulates Blood Sugar

The exact glycemic index of dragon fruit is not recorded but it is believed to have a glycemic index similar to that of banana ( 48 to 52).

Because of its low glycemic index, it releases sugar into the bloodstream at a slow pace, prevents a sudden spike in blood sugar level, and helps manage diabetes.

The dietary fibers in the dragon fruit slow down sugar absorption by the bloodstream and help in controlling blood sugar levels.

Vitamin C and other antioxidantal compounds like phytonutrients, polyphenols, carotenoids, thiols, tocopherols, and glucosinolates etc also plays a significant role in regulating blood sugar level.

As per a study published in the Journal of Pharmacognosy Research, antioxidantal compounds in dragon fruit combat against the oxidative stress from aortic stiffness, a common issue found in diabetic patients.

Consume dragon fruit in moderation as overeating it may drop the blood sugar to an abnormally low level giving rise to hypoglycemia with symptoms like shakiness, dizziness, excessive sweating, excessive hunger, fast heartbeat, confusion, and irritability or moodiness.

If you are on diabetic medication, consult your doctor before adding dragon fruit to your diet to avoid the risk of food-drug intolerance.

(5) Regulates Blood Pressure

The vasodilating properties of potassium in dragon fruit relaxes our blood vessels, improves blood circulation, and thus provides relief from high blood pressure or hypertension.

Hypertension is a silent killer that affects millions worldwide and gives rise to various health problems like severe headaches, nose bleed, chest pain, fatigue, irregular heartbeat, difficulty in breathing, vision problems, and blood in urine, etc.

Zinc and magnesium in dragon fruit also help in regulating high blood pressure.

Eat dragon fruit in moderation as high potassium level may lower the blood pressure to an abnormally low level giving rise to symptoms such as dizziness, fainting, nausea, dehydration, lack of concentration, blurred vision, and pale skin, etc.

If you are already on blood pressure medication, then consult your doctor before adding dragon fruit to your routine to avoid the risk of food-drug intolerance.

(6) Improves Cardiovascular Health

Dragon fruit improves our cardiovascular health as they contain antioxidants like vitamin C, vitamin A and other antioxidantal compounds like phytonutrients and polyphenols, etc that protect our cardiovascular system from free radical damage.

Dietary fibers in dragon fruit reduce LDL cholesterol and thus minimizes the risk of atherosclerosis which is a leading cause of several cardiovascular problems.

Potassium in dragon fruit regulates high blood pressure and minimize the risk of several cardiovascular and other health problems.

Monosaturated fats in dragon fruit improves our cardiovascular health.

Other nutrients like zinc, iron, magnesium, manganese, and copper, etc in dragon fruit also contribute to our cardiovascular health.

Dragon fruit aids in preventing and treating iron-induced anemia, a blood disorder caused by iron deficiency in the body. It gives rise to symptoms like fatigue, weakness, pale skin, irregular heartbeat, shortness of breath, dizziness, chest pain, and cold hands or feet, etc.
