35 Amazing Health & Beauty Benefits of Thyme

  • by Bharat Sharma
  • May 19, 2021

Thyme or “Thymus Vulgaris” is an evergreen shrub known for its medicinal and culinary purposes. It dates back to the Egyptian era and is native to the Mediterranean region and some parts of Africa. Being an excellent source of essential nutrients like vitamins, minerals, antioxidants, phytonutrients, polyphenols, and dietary fibers, etc, thyme provides numerous health and beauty benefits.

Some of the major health benefits of thyme are as follows

(1) Provides Antibacterial And Antifungal Benefits

Thymol, an organic compound in the thyme possesses anti-bacterial and anti-fungal properties and is very effective against various kinds of bacteria including E.coli. Thyme oil is effective in killing off bacteria both from the outside and inside.

Antioxidants like vitamin C, vitamin E, and other antioxidantal compounds like lutein, zeaxanthin, and thymosin, etc present in the thyme protects our cells and tissues from the oxidative stress caused by the free radicals and reduces the risk of various cancers.

Antioxidants also provide several other benefits like helping with detoxification, keeping vital organs like the liver and kidneys healthy, improving cardiovascular health, managing cholesterol level, strengthening immunity, improving cognitive functions, etc.

We will know more about these benefits later in this article.

Red blood cells play an important role in providing oxygenated blood to every cell and tissue of our body, nourishes them, and improves our health. Red blood cells improve blood circulation and also minimizes the risk of iron-induced anemia. 

Thyme also contains potassium, a mineral that relaxes our blood vessels and helps with blood circulation.

Consuming thyme in moderation keeps our cardiovascular system healthy due to the abundance of vital nutrients like vitamins, minerals, antioxidants, phytonutrients, polyphenols, and dietary fibers, etc. 

Antioxidants like vitamin C, vitamin E, and other relevant compounds like lutein, zeaxanthin, etc in thyme protect our cardiovascular system from free radical damage.

Dietary fibers in thyme reduce the level of bad LDL cholesterol and increase the level of good HDL cholesterol. This manages cholesterol levels and reduces the risk of atherosclerosis. 

Atherosclerosis is a condition that leads to hardening and narrowing of the arteries due to the accumulation of plaque and fat deposits in the arterial walls and is a leading cause of various cardiovascular problems like heart attack, heart stroke, and coronary artery disease.
